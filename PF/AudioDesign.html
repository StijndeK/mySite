Audio Design
I enjoy working on different types of music and sound design. As an audio designer I mostly work on Game Music, Sound Design and Implementation. I have also worked on different student movies, but over the last few years my main focus has been on games. What I find most important when working together with other media is that the result feels whole, which is why I try to be as adaptable as possible.

Implementation

As an audio programmer I enjoying doing most of my own game audio implementation. For this I mainly work with FMOD and Unity. The images show the progress of a soundtrack in FMOD and its system design for a (student) game I am currently working on.

What I love about adaptive audio is designing the adaptive systems together with game designers and experiencing the moment when the game and its audio click.

Composition

As a composer I have a lot of experience composing for smaller games at game jams or for student groups.

I originally started my studies as a film composer, but quickly came in touch with game composing. What really pulled me into composing for games are all the new possibilities and different systems. It also allows me to combine my passion for system design and programming with creating audio.

Sound Design

As a sound designer I am mainly experienced with synthesis but also have some foley studio experience. For synthesis I mainly use Massive (X), but I also have experience with other (digital) synths.

Most sound design I do is for games, which comes with some very interesting challenges like placing sounds in 3d spaces. What I love about doing sound design for games is making 'fake' worlds sound real and convincing and making sounds that don't actually exist in real life.

At the moment I do not have a lot of finished sound design projects, however I am working on sound designing a few games including a freerunning sci-fi game (see picture) and a fantasy game.
